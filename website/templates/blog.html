Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC)

Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC) is a type of epithelial skin cancer that seldom spreads but, if left untreated, can be fatal . It is the most common form of skin cancer.

Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC)

Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC) is a type of skin cancer that originates in the squamous cells of the skin that are noninvasive and can be treated locally without surgery.

Melanoma

Melanoma is the deadliest form of skin cancer, originating in the melanocytes and can take many different forms, the pigment-producing cells of the skin.
