Overview



Dermatofibroma (DF) is a common, benign skin lesion that typically appears as a firm, small nodule. Often found on the lower legs, these lesions are usually harmless but may raise cosmetic concerns for some individuals.

Characteristics and Types

  • Common Dermatofibroma: The typical form, presenting as a firm, round, and sometimes pigmented nodule usually less than 1 cm in diameter.
  • Atypical Dermatofibroma: Less frequently, these lesions may exhibit variations in color or texture while remaining benign.

Symptoms

  • Firm, raised nodule with a smooth or slightly dimpled surface
  • Often pigmented, ranging from light brown to dark brown or reddish tones
  • Generally asymptomatic, though some lesions may become tender or itchy

Causes



The exact cause of dermatofibromas is not fully understood. They are thought to result from minor skin trauma—such as insect bites or small injuries—which may trigger a localized fibrous tissue response.

Prevention

  • There are no definitive preventive measures, as dermatofibromas are benign and often related to minor skin injuries.
  • Maintaining good skin care and promptly treating minor wounds may help reduce their occurrence.

Treatment Options

  • Observation: Most dermatofibromas do not require treatment unless they cause discomfort or cosmetic concerns.
  • Surgical Excision: Removal may be recommended if the lesion is symptomatic or for definitive diagnosis.
  • Cryotherapy or Laser Treatment: Alternative options for lesion removal, though recurrence is possible.

When to See a Doctor

While dermatofibromas are benign, it is advisable to consult a dermatologist if you notice any changes in the size, shape, or color of a lesion, or if it becomes painful, itchy, or begins to bleed.
