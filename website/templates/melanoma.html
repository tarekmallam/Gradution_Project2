Overview



Melanoma is the most dangerous form of skin cancer, developing in the melanocytes—the pigment-producing cells of the skin. Although it is less common than other skin cancers, melanoma is more likely to spread to other parts of the body if not caught early.

Types of Melanoma

  • Superficial Spreading Melanoma: The most common type, typically appearing as an irregularly shaped mole that changes in color or size.
  • Nodular Melanoma: An aggressive form that often presents as a dark, raised bump and may grow rapidly.
  • Lentigo Maligna Melanoma: Often found in older adults on sun-damaged areas, particularly the face.
  • Acral Lentiginous Melanoma: Occurs on the palms, soles, or under the nails and is more common in individuals with darker skin tones.

Symptoms

  • Asymmetry in moles or spots
  • Irregular, scalloped, or poorly defined borders
  • Variation in color (different shades of brown, black, or sometimes red, blue, or white)
  • Diameter greater than 6mm
  • Evolution or change in a mole’s appearance

Causes



The primary cause of melanoma is excessive exposure to ultraviolet (UV) radiation from the sun or tanning beds. Genetic predisposition and a family history of melanoma can also contribute to the risk.

Prevention

  • Apply a broad-spectrum sunscreen with an SPF of 30 or higher daily.
  • Avoid prolonged exposure to the sun, especially during peak hours (10 AM to 4 PM).
  • Wear protective clothing, hats, and sunglasses when outdoors.
  • Avoid tanning beds and deliberate sunbathing.
  • Regularly examine your skin and consult a dermatologist for any suspicious changes.

Treatment Options

  • Surgical Excision: Removal of the melanoma along with a margin of healthy tissue.
  • Immunotherapy: Boosts the body’s immune system to help fight cancer cells.
  • Targeted Therapy: Uses drugs designed to target specific genetic mutations in melanoma cells.
  • Chemotherapy and Radiation Therapy: Often used for advanced cases.

When to See a Doctor

If you notice any changes in your skin, particularly in the appearance of moles or spots that become asymmetrical, have irregular borders, change in color, or increase in size, consult a dermatologist immediately.
